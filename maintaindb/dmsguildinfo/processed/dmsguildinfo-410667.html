Tier 3 [4-Hour Spelljammer Dungeoncraft]

The body of a dead god has mysteriously entered “Sector Eta,” a Wildspace system whose sun’s solar flares destroyed its planets. Contact with the divine body could spell apocalypse.

Can you prevent impact?

You Are My Sunshine is a four-hour action-packed adventure for characters of levels 11-16.

Get it on Roll20!

Save more with this Bundle!

Inside you’ll find:

  • A one-shot adventure that is easy to read and prepare. It is fast-paced, has 2 major parts, and is 6 pages long.
  • Guidance on how to adjust encounter difficulty (for different party compositions) from Very Weak to Very Deadly, and manage session time
  • 6 beautifully designed DM and player friendly handouts for spelljammer vessels, including their stats
  • Wildspace System Maps to help navigate Sector Eta
  • High quality DM and player friendly tactical battlemaps and spelljammer ship maps for easy printing and virtual tabletop use
  • 11 pieces of artwork to help bring the adventure to life
  • Stat blocks for all NPCs and monsters
  • An organized 42-page PDF containing all of the above, with separate compressed map and artwork files for easy use.
    • 6 pages: core adventure (quick and easy to read with dynamic elements for high-level adventuring)
    • 8 pages: stat blocks
    • 18 pages: artwork and map appendices
    • 10 pages: character rewards, DM miscellany, storytelling tactics and notes, etc.
  • A printer-friendly version of the document is included

CONTENT WARNING: Alcohol (Mention), Death (of a friend)

Adventurers League: This is a Spelljammer Adventure set in an original Wildspace system, created through the Adventurers League Dungeoncraft program. This is an Adventurers League legal Tier 3 Adventure.

[SPOILERS AHEAD] What your players will experience in You Are My Sunshine

Unfolding a Sinister Plot

The adventure begins on the Rock of Bral as the characters learn about Sector Eta, how the body of a dead god has mysteriously appeared in it, and the possible danger that contact could unleash. Astronomers have been dying, or so the rumors say. Is this just a coincidence? Let your players follow the threads and unravel this simple “fetch quest.”

High-level Spelljamming Ship Combat

On the way to Sector Eta, the characters’ commissioned Spelljammer Vessel named “The Snatcher” suddenly slows down. An ambush on the Astral Sea! The terrified crew cries out a name of a ghost ship that few have lived to tell the tale of. Let your players experience combat with an invisible pirate ship: “The Dutchman.”

An Ancient Evil

When the characters arrive at Sector Eta, they hear and feel a rhythmic foghorn-like blaring that pulses to the tune of a slowly beating heart. It's coming from the drifting body of a dead god. Who is behind the drifting of the dead god, Ligyron? What grand design is this for? Have your players’ characters encounter Vecna’s greatest follower, Lady Osterneth, known as "The Bronze Lich.”

Battle the Undead!

Your players will fight against the undead in two encounters: on the ghost ship with undead pirates on "The Dutchman," and against Osterneth and her undead crew. Perfect for Liar's Night and other events with a spooky atmosphere.

About the author: Brian’s love for the world of D&D started in 2000 with the PC game Baldur’s Gate, countless RA Salvatore novels about Drizzt and the companions, and naturally Baldur’s Gate II: Shadows of Amn. He started playing and running games for 5e in 2020. He enjoys running adventures with consequential player choices, a tight connected story, and a chance to learn more about the lore in D&D.

His first set of adventures, The Thin Veil Trilogy, involved reading more than 500 pages of materials from older editions to make sure that the way Planar Bleed and Planar Metaphysics, the Shadowfell, and Vecna were described were true to lore. Researching and finding ways to inject life into older stories is something he very much enjoys

    Finding the balance between "dangerous" and "adversarial" in T3 scaling is a fine art, and this author knows how to do it right. I had the extreme pleasure of playing this adventure in-person with the author, and I was kept on the edge of my seat for a proper high-tier challenge, but I never felt that the monsters or game design were arbitrarily over-tuned. Additionally, the story and NPC motivations are engaging in a way that is not present enough in high-tier content, making the non-combat portions of the adventure feel as important as the scenes where you duke it out.

  • David

    May 5, 2024

    I picked this up as I'll be running Destination Unknown & Soul & Shadow by the same author to help cap off my current campaign and this seemed like a good introduction to some of the concepts. It is, as avertised, really just the set piece to take characters between the main combats. The combats are creative, though I did have to beef them a little more than the precribed changes when scaling up (my Party ranked as "Very Strong" in the calcualtation).

    I was a little dissappointed that all of the artwork was created using AI (Midjourney). It's my fault for not reading the credits more carefully before purchase, because I'm strongly against AI art for profit and commerical use.

    I enjoyed the lore and am hopeful that the next two modules deliver on the story.

    I played this adventure with the author DMing at a virtual con. This adventure, as advertised, is mostly combat, and the combat is very challenging. However, the combat is much better than just a straight up slugging match. Most of the battles have secondary objectives, such as time limits or NPCs to protect, that help increase the tension as well as differentiate it from other modules. Would recommend for high-power groups that want a challenge.

  • Arthur

    Sep 29, 2022

    This adventure checks all the marks for a great T3 adventure: tt has both an epic plot with high stakes and an interesting villain.

    The adventure seems short, with two parts and a call to action, but combat takes a good chunk of this four hours adventure, which is a great thing since T3 characters are all about showing their cool toys, I mean features. There are a handful of appendix to help a DM run this adventure and the artwork has the right tone!

    My only critique is that I wish that the monster appendix was better organized. The author follows the standard DC guides and that is totally fine, but some monster stats are quite lengthy and I wonder if there would be a way to have used more page/column breaks so that monsters stats would be in a single column rather than across pages. For example, the stats for the BBEG are spread over two pages.
