description

A 4-hour Tier 1 adventure in a Feywild Domain of Delight

The perimeter of a volcanic island needs secured!

Help a nature preserve's new caretaker assure the area surrounding his base of operations is safe.

The Rookery is a magical tome containing a feywild demiplane dedicated to the preservation and rehabilitation of endangered species. The land inside consists of several islands containing
diverse habitats such that any creature needing care had terrain similar to home. Once the species recovered or flourished, they were reintroduced to their native plane.

Unfortunately, before the domain truly flourished, the book along with an entire Fey queenâ€™s library was banned and then lost. It was ultimately forgotten, and the land within it left to its own devices for many hundreds of years. As time passed, the original monitoring facilities became a forgotten ruin on a distant island.

The domain has recently been reclaimed and a new caretaker is rebuilding the former Visitor's Center. Now he needs help securing the rest of the island on which it resides, so he can start investigating the long lost domain.

The party will need to deal with a wild beast, assess an active volcano, locate a lost village, and uncover a terrible story.

This is designed as APL 3, but contains adjustment information to run at any level within Tier 1.

Inside you'll find:

  • An Adventurers League legal Tier 1 adventure.
  • 9 handouts and maps to help run the adventure
  • 48 page PDF

  

Run as an Adventurers League module, a one-shot, or in your own campaign!

    Having run this second adventure with the same group that ran through the first ones, the main criticism was around continuity. The whole adventure feels like individual missions being forced on a railroad without a good reason, why the railroad exists in the first place (there is no continuity thread in place which makes sense / could be felt). In the first adventure this was clearly stated from the outline (here are your five assignments, only if all are done we can safely start repairs), but here even the "you need to do this all, otherwise" aspect was missing.

    In general, as a DM I should have asked myself ahead of time "Why are the PCs even doing this and how does this tie into the bigger story I want to bring to live?"

    Options I would try if/when I run this for a second time.

    • The first encounter plus the random encounters. Instead of tying this into the railroad, have this be a first mini "workorder", where the PCs are asked to support food security, as the game around the rookery is dwindling and it does not make sense in the long run to ship things in from Elventree (something down the line to ensure there is a reason why)

    • Second encounter - Cling to the idea, this is why the PCs venture out in the first place and make it run as a fun engagement with roleplay.

    • Village - Similar to the idea / premise of the third adventure in the series, you could make the main reason to venture out finding what happened to the village (e.g. they read about it and want to make trade... whatever), and present this as the main goal /mission, allowing the adventurers to figure out for themselves that they probably need to check the second encounter to avoid harm to the Bastion of Rebirth.

    • Fourth encounter/part ... Feels like a work order to sustain the viablity of the Bastion. It makes a ton of sense from world building perspective and when looking into adventure three, yet taken on its own it feels like an afterthought. Two main issues:

      • Given the non violent nature of the players, I did allow them to avoid a fight (creatively they convinced the Evil NPC to look at the island in the north, see encounter five). Which worked (after all, still intelligent, sentient NPC and they had good arguments) yet in that case you have to be really careful how you master the main magical item into the hands of the players. As even though the village encounter feels like the main one, this last one is the one to fight to get the loot. It just felt bad for all of us. Especially given that the NPC in question is somebody you don't want to necessarily kill. Lets just say we did discuss this encounter in particular the longest.
      • How to better approach this? Make this encounter the first one of the Adventure three and move the loot ;-). Given the storyline, I would probably make it impossible for the PCs not to fight the "boss", make it clear "this is mine, I live here, you are trespassing, go die now".
      • It still lacks the "why" part. Unless we treat this as a separate work order, and then the mechanics of part one are better (they could just come back with their findings about the village and the school trip and the docks which exist and this ties into finding out more).

    • Last part.... No clue why. Neither why they should check it out nor why it is there in the first place. UNLESS you read the third adventure, then it makes sense that they have somebody and brought them back. You won't have time after the first four parts anyway, so I'd not run this again and pray that the adventure is still AL Legal without it.

    • UNLESS .... the work order is to retrieve a certain item/material from the other island that would help as it could only be harvested there (and is something on the other island which is needed for the Bastion to run) and you have to fight encounter four as it is guarding the docks where the only boat is still tied up and then the main magic item could be lying on the other island so it feels like an End with meaning.

    Don't get me wrong... I like the world building and the ideas, but in comparison to the first adventure, this does not explain the WHY to me. And given how long my brain had to work on ideas how to get to a WHY after the fact I'm not certain I would recommend this adventure as is to DMs to run with (hence my lengthy suggestions on what might work... which is MY opinion and is not play tested and should be taken with a grain of salt and is not intended to rewrite the story or minimize the appreciation to the work Chris put in). It feels odd to provide plotline suggestions publicly, but we are not consuming the plotline for ourselves, but to bring it to live and make it work for the players.
